Metal

Heavy metal, or simply metal, is a musical genre that was born late 1960s and early 1970s in the UK Kingdom and also in the United States, origins origins come from the blues rock, hard rock and psychedelic rock. Is characterized mainly because of his strong and distorted guitars, rhythms emphatic, the sounds of the bass and drums are thicker than expected. habitual and the voice is generally high-pitched or guttural.

Doom Metal

Doom metal generally uses the same instruments as the other genres derived from heavy metal: guitars, bass and drums. More traditional bands generally use a single guitarist, who acts as leader and rhythm at the same time. The other subgenres use two guitarists to give more power to their music, although this is not always the case. However, there are many bands (generally the most extreme) that intersperse the use of keyboards, as well as symphonic elements such as flutes, violins and harps. They are also many bands that add sounds generated by synthesizers, lately in the drone/doom bands. These, moreover, generally they dispense with the use of drums due to their long and dense tempos.
Imagen: Candlemass - Epicus Doomicus Metallicus

Thrash Metal

Thrash metal in general is characterized by the use of tempos faster, more aggressive guitar riffs and with a greater use of palm mute, quite fast solos but with many notes that escape the tonality, giving it a characteristic sound, bass sounds quite pronounced inherited from the NWOBHM. Something very special in style is the battery where pedal and bass drum alternate speeds above 180 bpm, while it is also worth of the double pedal or double bass drum and aggressive voices but without reaching the extreme of heavier styles like black metal, perhaps the fast and aggressive speed is more inherited from hardcore punk without However, thrash acquires more technique at the time of execution.
Imagen: Destroyer666 - Cold Steel... for an Iron Age

Black Metal

Traditional Black Metal has musical characteristics well defined: the voice is scratchy and sharp, known as shriek, the guitars are fast and the riffs are repetitive, whose usual technique is tremolo picking, and blast beats are the most used resource in the drums, and the bass for its part, is an instrument that passes background and is practically inaudible. In this genre, it prioritizes the creation of atmospheres in the compositions, over the their technical complexity. For this reason, the low quality of production and post-production is an important element that the first gangs used and is still in force, because, according to Varg Vikernes: 'A production that is too clean would fail to convey the feeling of coldness or desolation essential in Black Metal'
Imagen: UADA - Djinn

Death Metal

Death metal is an extreme subgenre of heavy metal. Is characterized for using guttural voices, guitars with an excessive use of overdrive, low tunings for your instruments, fast percussion (with a dominant use of the double pedal), complex structures music, as well as unorthodox techniques for the composition of the guitar riffs. The theme of death metal covers topics such as horror stories, anti-Christianity, serial killers, cannibalism, torture, among others. On the other hand, it is influenced by the physical and moral problems of man: death, warmongering, society, enlightenment, philosophy and others. Typically a band of death metal is made up of a vocalist, two guitarists, a bassist and a drummer, that is, the standard formation of heavy metal.
Imagen: 1914 - Where fear and weapons meet
